# Hey, I'm Ryan

I'm a software engineer based in **West Des Moines, Iowa** with a career spent building, breaking, fixing, and shipping software across a wild range of tech stacks. I currently wear two hats: one as a **Software Consultant at [Source Allies](https://www.sourceallies.com/)**, and the other as the entire IT department for **[Patient Preferred Services](https://patientpreferredservices.com/)**, a medical clinic payment and patient management platform.

I love the craft. I love the problem-solving. And I've always got time for learning something new.

## What I Do

### Husband & Father

First and foremost, I'm a father to a wonderful, spunky, and frightfully intelligent boy born in 2025 and a husband to the best, most beautiful, smart, and funny wife I could have met. My son would keep me busy on his own, but I gotta pay the bills and buy him and my wife ice cream when they need it, so I go to work doing the next two jobs.

### Source Allies

At Source Allies, I work as a Software Consultant, which means I'm leading projects, mentoring other engineers, reading and writing hundreds of lines of code a day, and focusing on delivering value at scale. The work spans full-stack development, cloud infrastructure, and building tools and services that help each client move faster and more reliably.

### Patient Preferred Services

Patient Preferred is a completely different beast. I'm the sole developer and IT person. I write all the software, maintain the cloud infrastructure on AWS, manage integrations with third-party software vendors, troubleshoot customer issues, and keep the lights on. The platform runs on a legacy .NET Framework API with an AngularJS frontend backed by SQL Server, and when I have time in off hours, I've been steadily modernizing the infrastructure with Terraform, Elastic Beanstalk, CloudFront, Lambdas, and RDS to reduce costs and improve reliability.

It's a lot of hats, but wearing them all has made me a significantly more well-rounded engineer.

#### Cost Reduction
Over three years, I've driven monthly AWS infrastructure costs from **~$1,400** down to **~$120**
(a roughly **91% reduction**) through incremental modernization with Terraform, CloudFront 
path-based routing, and migrating away from oversized EC2 instances, all while still improving availability, stability, and speed.

| Year | Monthly Cost | Change |
|------|-------------|--------|
| 2023 | ~$1,400 | — |
| 2025 | ~$600 | -57% |
| 2026 | ~$120 | -91% |
